# Programmatic SEO Safety Guide

## Purpose

Use this when evaluating scalable SEO pages such as location pages, product comparison pages, service-area pages, glossary pages, template pages, or AI-generated pages.

## Main risk

Programmatic SEO becomes risky when many pages are created with little unique value. This can create thin content, doorway pages, duplicate templates, index bloat, crawl waste, and weak user experience.

## Unsafe patterns

Avoid:
- thousands of pages with only city names swapped
- doorway pages made only to rank
- AI-generated pages without real data or human review
- scraped or spun content
- pages with no unique local/product/service value
- pages that target locations where the business does not operate
- pages with no internal linking plan
- pages created faster than they can be quality-checked

## Quality gates

Before publishing programmatic pages, each page type should have:
- clear search demand
- unique user intent
- unique content or data
- useful local/product/service details
- proof points
- internal links
- correct canonicals
- indexation rules
- schema where relevant
- analytics tracking
- sample review before scaling

## Safer scaling approach

1. Start with 10–20 test pages.
2. Validate indexation, engagement, clicks, conversions, and quality.
3. Improve the template.
4. Expand only to pages with real demand and unique value.
5. Noindex or consolidate weak generated pages.

## Location page rules

Good location pages include:
- real service availability
- local proof
- local testimonials or projects
- relevant FAQs
- service-area details
- map or coverage explanation
- unique title/H1
- internal links to related pages

Bad location pages:
- only swap city name
- use generic copy
- target locations with no service coverage
- exist only to capture traffic
- have no local proof

## Priority logic

Critical:
- Mass-generated pages are already indexed and harming important pages.

High:
- Thin programmatic pages target valuable commercial queries.

Medium:
- Small batch is being planned but not yet indexed.

Low:
- Pages are noindexed or used internally only.

## Default recommendation

Do not scale until one template is proven with real search demand, unique value, analytics, and quality checks.
